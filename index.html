üéØ Why Startup Cost Planning Matters

Underfunding is the #2 reason businesses fail (after lack of market need). Too many entrepreneurs launch with "just enough" ‚Äî then run out of cash before the business gains traction.

The goal isn't to minimize startup costs. It's to know EXACTLY what you need, so you're not surprised, stressed, or forced to close before you've really tried.

üí° The rule of thumb: Whatever you think you need, add 20-30% buffer. Unexpected costs ALWAYS appear.

üìê One-Time vs. Monthly Costs

Understanding the difference helps you plan both your launch AND your ongoing sustainability:

üí≥ One-Time Costs

Pay once to get started. These are your "launch" costs.

  • Business registration / LLC
  • Logo and brand design
  • Computer and equipment
  • Initial legal setup
  • Office furniture
  • Website development

üîÑ Monthly Costs

Recurring expenses that don't stop. Your "burn rate."

  • Software subscriptions
  • Rent / coworking
  • Insurance
  • Marketing / ads
  • Bookkeeper / accountant
  • Your living expenses

üéØ The Magic Number: 6-Month Runway

Most advisors recommend having 6 months of expenses saved before launching. This gives you time to find clients, make mistakes, and iterate ‚Äî without the panic of running out of cash next month.

üíº Pro Tips From the Field

  • Start lean, then invest. You don't need a fancy office or premium tools on day one. Start with the minimum, prove the concept, then level up.
  • Don't forget your living expenses. The #1 forgotten cost. You need to eat and pay rent while building the business. Include 6 months of personal expenses.
  • Add a 25% buffer. Something WILL cost more than expected. Build in cushion so surprises don't sink you.
  • Separate "need" from "nice." Logo? Need. Premium logo from a famous designer? Nice. Start with needs, add nice later.
  • Consider the "free" options first. Free website builders, free accounting tools, free email marketing (to a point). You can always upgrade.
  • Don't quit your job too early. If possible, start as a side hustle. Launch with paying clients, then make the leap with income already flowing.
